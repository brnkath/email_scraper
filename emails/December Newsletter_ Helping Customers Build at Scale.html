re:Invent: Heroku and AWS Expand Partnership

Day two at AWS re:Invent featured an exciting announcement about the expanded partnership with Salesforce and AWS. This announcement makes a range of Heroku and Salesforce products available for purchase through the AWS Marketplace Private Offers program, giving our shared customers new ways to manage their cloud investments. You can also look forward to future innovations as we partner to leverage AWS Graviton, AWS Trainium, AWS Inferentia, AWS Aurora, and developer services like Amazon CodeWhisperer to deliver AI assistants to your developers and operators. Day three brought even more excitement as Heroku CTO, Gail Frederick took the stage with AWS leaders for a special session on Future-proofing your applications.
 
 

 

Enhancing Heroku Postgres with pgvector: Generating AI Insights

In November, Heroku delivered the highly-requested, pgvector extension on Heroku Postgres. In an era where large language models (LLMs) and AI applications are paramount, pgvector can perform high-dimensional vector similarity searches. Read this in depth blog from Heroku engineer Valerie Woolard as she walks through how to work with vector data and covers important optimization and performance considerations.

The Future of Multi-Architecture Cloud Native Buildpacks

One aspect of Heroku’s expanded partnership with AWS is our goal to incorporate tools like AWS Graviton that offer the best price-performance compute. ARM processing provides great efficiency, but it will require buildpack developers to prepare for multi-architecture development. Check out this KubeCon talk with Terence Lee and Joe Kutner. They discuss the challenges of supporting multi-architecture application images, Salesforce's journey with Hyperforce, and how Buildpacks resolve them.
